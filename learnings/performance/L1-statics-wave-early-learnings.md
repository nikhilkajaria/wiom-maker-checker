## The learnings

1. **Anchor — Hindi "घर का नेट" pulls cheaper than English "Home net".** 🔶 Hook tied (CTR 0.50 vs 0.49), but it installs at **₹18 vs ₹24**. → Use "Ghar ka net". *(T-041 vs T-042)*
2. **Format must fit the idea — it flips by theme.** 🔶 A simple scene wins as a **static** (0.48% vs 0.23% as a carousel). A "WiFi नहीं" claim wins as a **carousel** (0.48% vs 0.36% as a static).
3. **Curiosity hooks; a scene doesn't.** 🔶 Same format (both carousels, card 1): WiFi-नहीं **0.48%** vs a plain family scene **0.23%**. The negation opens a loop; a happy family is just a state.
4. **Curiosity wins attention; clarity wins bookings.** 🔶 The clean इशारों-पे static — which **shows the recharge day-options on the image** — books cheapest of all 27 live ads (**₹1,366, #1**). But "WiFi नहीं" as a **lone static** is the worst at booking (**₹9,602, #23**) — it grabs attention, but one frame can't explain it. As a carousel it converts fine.
5. **The pre-launch scorecard isn't predictive yet.** 🔶 The highest-scored creative was the pool's worst at scale — watch whether the rubric over-rewards craft and under-prices misread risk (validate across ~30+ creatives).

**Headline:** *curiosity wins attention, clarity wins bookings — and the "WiFi नहीं" idea only converts in carousel form, never as a lone static.*

## Next steps

### Do now (no test needed)
1. Write **"घर का नेट"** (Hindi), not "Home net" — it reached people cheaper.
2. Run **"ये WiFi नहीं"** only as a 2-card carousel, never a lone image.
3. Show the **recharge day-options on the image itself**, every time.

### Tests to run (each changes one thing)
- **Test 1 (first) — Why does "WiFi नहीं" grab attention, the picture or the words?** A: crossed-WiFi *picture* + plain words · B: curious *words* + no crossed picture. → whichever wins on clicks is what to reuse.
- **Test 2 — Why does इशारों-पे book cheapest, the message or the element?** Same picture in all three: A = as-is (line + WiFi-symbol element); B = same line + calendar element; C = same element + plain line. → A vs B = element? A vs C = message?
- **Test 3 — More curiosity carousels.** 3–4 carousels, different curious card-1, same card-2 (घर का नेट · 1/7/14 din). → which hook pulls best.
- **Test 4 — More comparison ads.** 2–3 more split-screen "this vs that" ads. → does comparison keep winning attention.

### Before scaling spend on any winner
1. **Week 2–3:** do the cheap bookings actually **recharge**? (A booking that never recharges is a fake win.)
2. **Misread check:** ask a few people "what is this ad for?" — nobody should say "mobile recharge."
