# Content Learnings — for the Creative Director

**Prepared:** 2026-07-16, by Shiva + creative-data-analyst agent
**Data window:** 2026-06-30 → 2026-07-13 (14 completed days), Meta only
**Spend floors applied:** ≥ ₹5,000 (Delhi), ≥ ₹1,500 (Bharat) per creative
over the window; only creatives active in the last 3 days were ranked
**Creatives content-read:** T-063, T-064, T-082 (statics); C-069, C-076,
C-077, H-002, H-004 (videos) — 8 of the 9 nominated by the data run
([report.md](report.md))

**Method, stated honestly:** the data nominated creative *pairs* whose
numbers diverge sharply; we then compared what is visibly/verbally on
screen. Only stark, observable differences are reported — no taste calls.
Video reads are from 12 sampled frames + on-screen supers per film;
**voice-over was NOT transcribed** — anything VO-only is invisible to this
read. Portfolio benchmarks (p50/p90) are at the end; "beats p90" means
top-decile of its own pool.

**One structural fact that strengthens every video learning:** all five
videos end with the SAME end card (जितने दिन रिचार्ज, उतने दिन नेट + Wiom
+ rating + CTA) and the same app day-picker insert. The endings are
constant — so conversion differences between videos must come from the
opening and middle.

## Learnings (each = campaign · objective · pool · window · spend stated)

### L1 — Headline: a mechanism-statement converts better than a
### challenge-question, on the SAME image
**Campaign:** BFC-VOLUME (optimized for app installs) · Delhi + Bharat
statics · Jun 30–Jul 13 · combined spend ₹3.57L (T-063: ₹2.88L, T-064:
₹0.70L)

T-063 and T-064 are **pixel-identical** — same comparison layout, same two
routers, same bullets, same CTA — except one line:
- T-063 headline: **"महीने का बंधन क्यों?"** (challenge-question)
- T-064 headline: **"जितनी ज़रूरत, उतना रिचार्ज"** (mechanism-statement)

| | CTR Delhi | CVR Delhi | CTR Bharat | CVR Bharat |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| T-063 (question) | **0.576** | 9.2 | **0.733** | 18.0 |
| T-064 (statement) | 0.512 | **11.0** | 0.711 | **22.7** |

Same direction in both geos: the question buys slightly more clicks; the
statement converts clicks to installs better (CPI ends up equal-or-better
for the statement: ₹60.0 vs ₹64.7 Delhi; ₹21.1 vs ₹22.2 Bharat).
**Direction for next statics:** when the goal is installs, put the
mechanism in the headline; use question-hooks only when buying attention
is the objective.

### L2 — The home-anchor: remove "घर" + the router, and clicks stop
### converting
**Campaign:** BFC-VOLUME (app installs) · Delhi statics · Jun 30–Jul 13 ·
T-082 spend ₹15.4K vs T-064 ₹60.1K

T-082 and T-064 get clicked at nearly the same rate (CTR 0.552 vs 0.512)
but T-064 converts those clicks at ~2× (CVR 11.0% vs 5.8%; CPI ₹60 vs
₹119.5). The observable content differences are stark:

| | T-082 | T-064 |
|---|---|---|
| The word **घर** | **absent** — headline says "Net जितना use करो" | present twice — "घर का नेट" subhead + "रिचार्ज वाला घर का नेट" label |
| Product visual | none — abstract WiFi icon on black | two physical routers (old locked vs Wiom) |
| Contrast/enemy | none — benefit statement only | named enemy: "चलाओ या ना चलाओ, पूरा बिल / Fixed monthly बिल" |
| Category label | absent | "रिचार्ज वाला घर का नेट" |
| Day options | present (1/7/14 दिन) | absent |

Candidate mechanism (consistent with the known "recharge = mobile
top-up" misread from CX calls): without any home/router anchor,
"Net + recharge" language collects mobile-data clickers who bounce on the
Play-Store page. Note T-082 *does* list day options — so day-listing alone
does not convert; the home-category anchor is the separating element.
**Direction:** every install-objective static must carry at least one
home anchor (the word घर / the router / the category label). Test which
anchor is load-bearing (see proposed test in §Next tests).

### L3 — Videos: the entry frame decides WHO clicks, and that decides
### conversion
**Campaign:** BFC-VOLUME (app installs) · Delhi videos · Jun 30–Jul 13 ·
C-069 ₹74.1K, C-077 ₹6.3K (low-n caution on C-077: 50 installs)

Identical end cards, opposite entries:
- **C-077** (best attention: CTR 0.443, hold 23.1; worst honesty: CVR
  11.1) opens inside the **mobile-data story**: "पहले घर में नेट सिर्फ मेरे
  फोन से चलता था… बेटे की ऑनलाइन क्लास, वाइफ का… तो रोज शाम तक खत्म" —
  street setting, expressive testimonial.
- **C-069** (worst hook: 8.3%; best honesty: CVR 16.9, CPI ₹87.8) opens by
  **qualifying the viewer in second one**: "घर का net चाहिए? पर पूरे महीने
  का plan भारी लगता है?" — then explains the mechanism (newspaper analogy
  → day-picker UI).

The mobile-data entry is the more arresting story AND collects the
wrong-intent click; the qualifying-question entry repels casual scrollers
AND pre-filters for home-net intent. Caveat to hold: C-077 is L3, C-069 is
L2 (different layer labels) — flagged to Shiva below.
**Direction:** decide per film whether its job is reach or installs; for
installs, open by naming the home-net situation, not the mobile-data pain.

### L4 — Bharat: city-name callout + real local creator = 2× the hook of
### an AI narrator; then naming the enemy converts the attention
**Campaign:** BFC-VOLUME (app installs) · Bharat videos · Jun 30–Jul 13 ·
H-004 ₹32.2K combined, H-002 ₹24.7K combined, C-076 (Bharat) ₹1.7K

Same pool, same end cards:
- **H-002** (Bareilly creator, park): opens "बरेली में हर घर की एक problem
  है" — hook 25.2%.
- **H-004** (Agra creator, Agra Fort backdrop, creator logo): opens "आगरा
  वालों आज मुझे एक ऐसी चीज़ मिली है" — hook 24.9–26.8%.
- **C-076** (AI narrator, generic street, no place named): hook 11.2%.

The two creatives that name a city in line one and stand in a
recognizable local place more than double the 3-second stop rate vs the
same message without place (11% → 25%+).

Within the creator format, the conversion gap is also content-visible:
**H-004 names the enemy and the offer in supers** ("घर की नेट का monthly
bill वाला पूरा चक्कर ही बदल देगी", "TV, laptop, phone", "तो recharge मत
करो") and converts at CVR 21.0% at scale; **H-002 stays on curiosity**
("हर घर की एक problem है… सबको लगवाना है… कैसे?") — the mechanism appears
only in the app-UI insert — and converts at 12.2% at scale.
**Direction for Bharat films:** open with the city's name + local
creator/landmark; spend the middle naming the monthly-bill enemy and the
concrete mechanism, not extending the mystery.

### L5 — The account's most honest message is wearing its weakest opener
**Campaign:** BFC-VOLUME (app installs) · both geos · Jun 30–Jul 13 ·
C-076 spend ₹7.3K Delhi + ₹1.7K Bharat (low spend — direction, not
decimals)

C-076's script is a considerer's confession: "सच कहूँ… नेट लगवाने का तो
कबसे सोच रहा था… पर हर बार यही लगता था… कोई monthly plan नहीं है." Its CVR
is the best in the account in BOTH geos (25.1% Delhi, 41.9% Bharat — beats
both pools' p90). Its opening 3 seconds: a plain mid-shot man on a generic
street, first super "सच कहूँ" — no place, no question, no product, no
motion device. Hook: 11–13%, worst tier.
**Direction:** this is the highest-expected-value edit in the account —
keep the entire confession script, replace only the opening seconds with a
proven hook device (L4's city callout, or L3's qualifying question).
**Also observable:** on-screen typo **"montly plan"** — fix in any re-cut.

## Cross-cutting observations (facts, not calls)

1. **Every high-CVR creative contains an explicit home-category anchor**
   (घर का नेट said or shown) — T-064, C-069, C-076, H-004. The two CVR
   laggards read (T-082, C-077) either drop the anchor or enter through
   the mobile-data frame.
2. **The app day-picker UI appears in all five videos** — it cannot
   explain differences between them, and its presence in T-082 (worst
   static CVR) shows day options alone don't convert.
3. **RETARGETING (optimized for bookings, warm audience):** none of its
   creatives were in the folder, so no content read. Data note stands: the
   BFC winners T-063/T-064 rank bottom of that pool while T-043 ranks top
   — do not assume these L1–L5 learnings transfer; treat any transfer as a
   hypothesis to re-prove.
4. **AWARENESS (optimized for ThruPlay):** no creatives in folder; its
   hooks (p50 38%, best 52%) are far above BFC's — worth a separate
   content read to extract opening devices, then test them in BFC films.
