## The constant objective

Every gate exists to protect two things and avoid one misread:

- **Protect:** the *ghar ka net* category anchor, and the *recharge / pay-as-you-need* shape.
- **Avoid:** the **mobile-data-product misread** (the primary dangerous misread). Broadband
  and cheap-WiFi misreads are secondary.

## Why the rules flex by layer

Each layer starts from a different mental frame, so the *same word* carries different
contamination risk:

| Layer | Who they are | Starting frame | Mobile-data-misread risk | Strictness |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **L1** | Ready buyer — already shopping home internet, comparing Jio/Airtel/local broadband | WiFi / broadband | **Low** (they live in the WiFi world) | **Lenient** |
| **L2** | Blocked buyer — wants home internet but monthly-commitment shape doesn't fit | commitment / cost | Medium | **Moderate** |
| **L3** | Workaround pain — surviving on mobile data / hotspot | mobile data / top-up | **High** (they live in the mobile world) | **Strict** |

The dial loosens **L3 → L1**: the more a viewer already lives in the WiFi frame, the
freer we are to use the market's words.

## Gate 1 — Category-Frame Integrity (binary)

**Core requirements — present at ALL layers (this IS the anti-contamination floor):**

1. **Ghar anchor present.** Household / whole-home is explicit or unmistakable, in visual or audio.
2. **Recharge-shape present.** Pay-as-you-need behaviour is conveyed, not buried.

**Fail test:** *"If a reviewer must supply the ghar anchor themselves to make the ad cohere, it fails."*

**Layer-tuned bits:**

| Rule | L1 | L2 | L3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **JDRUDN locked anchor** ("Jitne din recharge, utne din net") appears verbatim | encouraged | required | required |
| **"No frame drift"** (must not land as cheap WiFi / flexible mobile data / budget broadband) | lenient — fails only on an *outright* category break | moderate | strict |

**JDRUDN in statics — clarification (proposed Nikhil, 26-Jun-2026):**
For STATIC creatives the verbatim JDRUDN line is NOT mandatory. A static that communicates the
recharge rule and benefit through its own copy (comparison rows like "jab marzi tab recharge" /
"jitna chalao utna recharge", duration chips, etc.) satisfies recharge-shape; JDRUDN variations
are allowed. The locked verbatim form is required only when JDRUDN appears as a proposition /
lockup unit (a footer line beside the CTA). Do NOT iterate/fail a static solely for a missing
verbatim JDRUDN footer. (Video end-slates keep the verbatim requirement.)

**Constraint-first / MCR framing is NOT contamination (proposed Nikhil, 26-Jun-2026):**
Surfacing the monthly-commitment constraint (e.g. "mahine ka bandhan kyun?") is the L2/MCR
need-state and a valid constraint-first (CF) opening — CF is the wave's default structure, so
leading with the constraint cannot itself be a fail. A GENERIC category comparison (Wiom recharge
vs "monthly plan", no named competitor, no price/speed/Mbps terms) passes. Frame-drift applies
only when the creative (a) names a competitor (Jio / Airtel / ACT), or (b) argues on broadband's
price / speed terms. Do NOT kill a constraint-first or comparison creative merely for leading with
the monthly-commitment problem.

## Gate 2 — Contamination / Banned Words (binary)

**Banned (kill-list) words:** WiFi, broadband, fiber, speed plans, cheap internet, ISP.

The test is **role, not presence** — *bridge* (the user's own starting word) vs. *identity*
(defining what Wiom IS).

### Universal hard floor — fails at EVERY layer, no softening

- A banned word **defines what Wiom IS** — e.g. "Wiom ek WiFi service hai", "best cheap WiFi",
  "WiFi jaisa par sasta". (Identity contamination.)
- The creative's **destination / closing noun** lands on "recharge" or a banned word instead
  of *net / ghar ka net*.
- The dominant read is **mobile-data top-up** with no ghar anchor recoverable.

### Layer dial — everything softer flexes

| Tolerance | L1 | L2 | L3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Banned word in the **user's own voice** at the hook (then converts to RWGKN) | freely allowed | allowed | allowed only with a rock-solid ghar + category anchor *first* |
| **Comparison framing** (Jio/Airtel/broadband as the user's starting point) | allowed | watch for drift into "flexible mobile data" | not allowed before anchor |
| Recharge-led / WiFi-led **hook before the category lands** | tolerated | discouraged | **fails** |

**The L1 rule in one line:** *pass unless it outright (a) breaks the ghar-ka-net category,
or (b) misuses a banned word as Wiom's identity.* Everything softer passes at L1.

**Guardrail (all layers):** presence of "WiFi" alone is **never** an automatic fail. Always
check the role the word plays — bridge (PASS) vs. identity (FAIL). Over-rejection kills
legitimate capture-bridge creative.

**WiFi GLYPH vs the WORD "WiFi" (proposed Nikhil, 26-Jun-2026):** the kill-list targets the
WORD "WiFi" used as Wiom's identity. The WiFi glyph / symbol is the LOCKED visual depiction of
ghar ka net (per the creative global rules — ghar ka net = WiFi symbol, never mobile-signal bars)
and is ALLOWED. Never flag the WiFi symbol / icon in a visual description as a G2 violation.

## Verdict mapping (applies after both gates run)

- **PASS** — G1 and G2 both pass. → human approval → production-ready brief assembly.
- **ITERATE** — a gate fails on a **structural, fixable** issue (a four-word add or single-line
  rewrite would fix it — e.g. missing ghar anchor at close; one banned word used as identity
  at close). → returns to the maker with a specific structured fix.
- **KILL** — a gate fails on a **conceptual** issue: the concept is built on the wrong frame
  (the whole creative is a mobile top-up; the premise is "Wiom is cheap WiFi"; the mobile-data
  misread is the most likely read and no small edit fixes it). → logged with reason, not iterated.

**Default bias is KILL, not nurture.** Activation creative is cheap to produce; a marginal
creative kept alive "to see" accumulates contamination. A concept that does not clearly earn
PASS or ITERATE is KILLed.
