Facebook Performance Ads
The goal of a Facebook ad is not to explain the product. The goal is: get the right person to stop scrolling, understand why they should care, understand why your solution is different, and know what to do next.
SECTION A
Wiom Brand Rules
These rules apply to every creative, before evaluation begins. A creative that breaks any of these is disqualified regardless of its score.
A1: Positioning
Wiom — Recharge Wala Ghar ka Net
Pay-As-You-Go home internet. No one else in India offers this.
Category rule
Jitne din Recharge, utne din Net
This is the one line that defines the category. Every creative must communicate this rule, in these words or a faithful variant.
Every creative must make Wiom feel like a new category, not a better version of existing internet options. The recharge mechanic is the differentiation. It must be visible, not just implied.

Always accompanied by the Play Store icon.

A5: Comparison Ad Rules
Wiom can run comparison ads. One rule governs all of them: the destination must be Wiom's category, not just Wiom's name.

A6: Visual Direction
The user is the hero. Never the product.
The creative shows a person whose life got better. The product is the enabler in the background, if visible at all.
What this means in practice
Element
Direction
Subject
Show the moment of benefit: kids on a video class, family watching together, a man recharging for exactly the days he needs. Not the device, not the app UI as centrepiece.
Casting
Relatable lower and middle income Indian household. Real homes with real clutter, not aspirational apartments. The viewer should think 'yeh mere jaisa hai'.
Emotion
Relief and control, not tech excitement. The face tells the story of bill anxiety gone.
Product presence
Wiom logo, recharge options and CTA live in the text layer. The router, if shown, sits naturally in the scene, never centre frame.
Setting
Inside the home, daily life in progress. The home is the stage, the user is the actor, internet is the invisible enabler.
Anti-patterns (auto-fail the visual)
Speed-test screenshots or Mbps numbers as the visual hero
Glowing routers, signal waves, fibre cables, data-centre imagery
Stock-photo corporate families in premium apartments
City skyline or abstract tech backgrounds
App UI screenshots as the primary image
Visual test
Remove all text from the creative. Does the image still say 'an ordinary household whose life works better'? If the image only says 'internet product', the visual fails.
A7: Mobile Legibility
Creatives are viewed on phones, in a feed, for roughly 1.5 seconds. The framework scores what an ad says; this rule limits how much it says. A creative that fails any rule below is reworked before scoring, regardless of how strong its copy is.
Rule
Detail
One idea, once
No message may appear twice on the canvas. If the category rule is in the mechanic band, it cannot also sit under the CTA button. Repetition is clutter, not reinforcement.
Text budget: 6 elements max
Headline, one subline, one mechanic line, recharge options, CTA button, logo lockup. Anything beyond this moves to the ad caption.
3-second phone test
View the creative at actual phone size for 3 seconds. Whatever could not be read gets deleted or enlarged. Never shrunk to fit.
Smallest text rule
If a line must be small to fit, it does not belong on the image. Proof points and CTA riders go to the caption.
Caption is part of the ad

SECTION B
Target Personas
Wiom targets lower and middle income Indian households. Within that, two buyer states determine what a creative needs to do.
Targeting cannot be controlled at creative level.
Both L1 and L2 may see any creative. The default must be creatives with range — hooks that work for someone not yet shopping, not just someone already comparing options.
L1 — Ready Buyer
Dimension
Detail
Who
Actively looking for home internet. Will buy from someone — Wiom needs to be the answer.
Mental state
Comparing options. Price-aware. Weighing commitment vs cost.
What the creative must do
Show differentiated value. Recharge flexibility is the hook. Make them feel Wiom fits their life better than a fixed plan.
Core message
Recharge as per your life. Jab chahein, jitne din chahein.
Hook type
Differentiation and comparison. 'Yeh WiFi nahin, Wiom hai' works here.
L2 — Locked Buyer
Dimension
Detail
Who
Would like home internet but has opted out. Irregular cash flow. Cannot commit to monthly or quarterly payments.
Mental state
Door is mentally closed. Not scrolling for internet options. Current category doesn't fit their life.
What the creative must do
Crack a locked belief. Name the exact reason they opted out. Make them feel the barrier has been removed.
Core message
Ghar ka net ab commitment ke bina. Jitne din Recharge, utne din Net.
Hook type
Life-situation pain. Not comparison. Comparison assumes they are already in the market.
Dual-Persona Stress Test
Because L1 and L2 cannot be separated at delivery, every creative must pass this test before scoring:
Test 1 — Range
Would this creative stop someone who is NOT currently looking for home internet? If no, it is a narrow L1-only creative.
Test 2 — Confusion
Would this creative confuse someone who has never considered home internet? If yes, it risks alienating L2.
Creative type
Range
Life-situation hook (pain/cash flow/mobile data)
Both L1 and L2 — default format
Recharge mechanic as hero
Both L1 and L2 — strong default
Comparison / category contrast
Primarily L1 — use as secondary format
Brand feature list
L1 only — weakest for range
