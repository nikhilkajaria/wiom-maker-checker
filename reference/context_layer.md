## 1. What Wiom is

Wiom is building **Recharge-wala Ghar ka Net** — the first recharge-shaped home-internet category for Bharat households. This is **not** "affordable WiFi," "cheap broadband," or "mobile data for home." It is whole-home internet with recharge-shaped commitment behaviour.

The strategic user is the **first-time home-internet household graduating from mobile data** — not the broadband switcher. They understand recharge, packs, top-ups, daily/28-day prepaid behavior, and hotspot jugaad. They do not always understand "WiFi," "broadband," "ISP plans," "speed tiers," or "fixed monthly commitment."

Both halves of the category are co-equal:
- Remove "ghar" → it becomes mobile data.
- Remove "recharge-shaped" → it becomes broadband.

## 2. The three memory anchors (locked, verbatim)

| Rung | Anchor | Job |
|------|--------|-----|
| Label | **Recharge-wala Ghar ka Net** (RWGKN) | Names the category |
| Rule | **Jitne din recharge, utne din net** (JDRUDN) | States the operating logic (invariant — same for every user) |
| Emotion | **Chale aapke hisaab se** | Names how it feels (user agency) |

These must appear in their locked forms when used. They are category-memory assets, not campaign copy.

**Bridge benefit (flexible articulation, contextual per need-state):**
- **Jitni zarurat, utna recharge** (JZUR) — explains *why the rule is useful*. JZUR is not a fourth rung; it sits between the rule and the emotional anchor as the connective benefit line. It is a benefit template that flexes per need-state:
  - Exam season: "exam ke 15 din ka recharge"
  - Match day: "match wale din ka recharge"
  - Salary delay: "abhi 2 din, salary aane pe poora mahina"
  - Family travel: "ghar par nahi, toh recharge nahi"

**Rule vs benefit — SD's framing (use the analogies verbatim with the team):**
- **JDRUDN = the RULE.** Tells people *how this thing works*. Like "Ticket kharido → movie dekho." Or "Button dabao toh paani niklega." Very specific, unique operating logic.
- **JZUR = the BENEFIT.** Tells people *why this rule is useful*. Like "Paise waste nahi honge." Or "Jab pyaas lage tab use karo." Emotional / value explanation.
- **Both are needed in creatives.** Rule alone leaves no felt benefit; benefit alone leaves no understanding of how. The locked rule (JDRUDN) appears verbatim as the memory anchor; the benefit (JZUR-shaped) appears inside the body to explain why the rule matters.

**Underlying principle: lock the constant, flex the contextual.**
- The rule is locked because it is **invariant** — JDRUDN is the same operating logic for every user, every zone, every need. It never changes, so it is the thing repeated until it lodges.
- The benefit is flexed because it is **contextual** — JZUR is a template each need-state fills in differently. Same benefit logic, different clothing per situation.

**Three-rung guard.** Keep the full three-anchor stack intact: label (RWGKN) / rule (JDRUDN) / emotion (Chale aapke hisaab se). JZUR is the bridge between rule and emotion — it does not displace "Chale aapke hisaab se" (which does a different job: how-it-feels / user agency). Three rungs, three jobs. JZUR is the connective, not a fourth rung.

## 3. The dangerous misreads (priority-ordered)

| Priority | Misread | Why it's dangerous |
|----------|---------|---------------------|
| **PRIMARY** | **Mobile-data product** | Already showing up in mid-funnel. Daily recharge + ₹23/day sounds like a mobile top-up. The "ghar" anchor is what prevents it. |
| Secondary | Broadband / fiber comparison | Pulls users to compare Wiom vs Jio/Airtel on speed, Mbps, monthly plan terms. |
| Secondary | Cheap WiFi | Pulls users to compare on price-as-low-quality. Reduces Wiom to commodity. |

When weighting misread risk, **mobile-data-product is the primary misread to weight hardest**. The Context Note names it as the dangerous one. Broadband and cheap-WiFi misreads are secondary.

## 4. The capture / convert split (Principles §2)

**Capture using the market's words. Convert using Wiom's category.**

Users may search "WiFi." We meet them at that word — but Wiom must anchor them into Recharge-wala Ghar ka Net. WiFi is a discovery bridge. It is never the category identity.

**Allowed (capture):**
- Bidding on "home wifi near me"
- An ad opening "WiFi lene jaa rahe ho?"
- A vox-pop respondent saying "main WiFi dhoondh raha tha"
- The word is in the **user's own mouth**, at the hook/opening, as the language the in-market user already uses

**Not allowed (identity):**
- "Wiom — best cheap WiFi"
- "Wiom ek WiFi service hai"
- "WiFi jaisa, par sasta"
- Any framing that asks the user to compare Wiom against Jio / Airtel / broadband on their terms
- The word becomes Wiom's **category identity**, which pulls the user back into the old frame

## 5. Scope: creative vs landing page

Per the three-stage activation model (Activation → Landing → Measurement):

| Stage | Job |
|-------|-----|
| Activation creative | Capture active intent. Commitment-break framing, household tension, recharge-shaped relief. Capture at the market's words. |
| Landing page | Aggressively re-anchor into RWGKN — ghar + recharge-shape, above the fold. |
| Measurement | Protect quality. Track cohorts separately; scale only on recharge quality. |

A 15-second activation creative's job is to **establish** the ghar anchor and recharge-shape and **begin** category anchoring — not to fully resolve mental-model conversion. Full category comprehension is completed downstream at the landing page (which is outside this system's scope).

G1 must therefore not be tuned to demand that a short creative fully resolve comprehension on its own.

## 6. Gate 1 — Category-frame integrity (binary)

**Passes only if all three hold:**

1. **Ghar anchor present.** Household / whole-home is explicit or unmistakable — in visual or audio. Non-optional.
2. **Recharge-shape clear.** Pay-as-you-go / recharge behavior is conveyed, not buried.
3. **No frame drift.** The creative does not land as "cheap WiFi," "flexible mobile data," or "budget broadband."

**Fail test:** "If a reviewer must supply the ghar anchor themselves to make the ad cohere, it fails."

**Locked-anchor minimum:** JDRUDN (Jitne din recharge, utne din net) should appear verbatim at least once (typically end-slate or as a summary line). Other articulations of the mechanic and the benefit are encouraged (visual demos, dialogue walkthroughs, multi-person patterns, situational explanations) so creatives do not read as templated. But the locked anchor must land somewhere in the creative.

## 7. Gate 2 — Contamination / kill-list (binary)

**Kill-list words:** WiFi, broadband, fiber, speed plans, cheap internet, ISP.

**Two distinct checks:**

1. **Capture-bridge use (ALLOWED):** A kill-list word appears in the user's own voice, at the hook/opening, as the language the in-market user already uses — followed by conversion to RWGKN. The word is the bridge that meets the user where they are.

2. **Identity use (FORBIDDEN):** A kill-list word is used to define or describe Wiom itself. Here the word becomes Wiom's category identity.

**Fail test:** "Does the creative invite the user to compare Wiom against Jio/Airtel/broadband on their terms? If a kill-list word defines what Wiom IS (rather than what the user was looking for), it fails. If it only names the user's starting point and the creative then converts to RWGKN, it passes."

**Common error to avoid:** Do NOT blanket-fail any creative containing "WiFi." Over-rejection kills legitimate capture-bridge creative. Check the *role* the word plays — bridge (PASS) vs identity (FAIL).

## 8. Audience layers (Messaging System v1.3 — LOCKED)

Three layers, three jobs. Every creative targets exactly one layer. The checker must consider which layer a concept targets and apply layer-specific rules accordingly.

### L1 — Ready buyer
"I want home WiFi now." Active, comparison-mode, incumbent-trained (Jio/Airtel mindset).

**Differentiator to land:** FLEXIBILITY — pay only for days you use. Granularity is the proof.

**Bridge to build:** "Which WiFi brand do I pick" → "this is a different way to have home internet" → "flexibility is why it's worth choosing."

**Must NOT do (L1):**
- Lead with WiFi as a standalone noun without a strong RWGKN destination — traps the user inside broadband schema
- Compare to Jio / Airtel / ACT by name — accepts their frame
- Add occasion examples (match, exam) before category is anchored — May sprint: Static 02 = 21% confusion when category unanchored
- Promise "cheaper" — commoditises into broadband price war
- Cram category and flexibility into one beat — one job per creative

**Contamination risk:** L1 is the highest-contamination layer (burst-then-churn signal). Scale only on recharge quality; cohort-fail overrides comprehension-pass.

---

### L2 — Blocked buyer
"I want home internet but its current shape doesn't fit my life." Natively fit — Wiom IS their answer.

**Differentiator to land:** COMMITMENT-SHAPE — pay-as-you-go, no monthly lock-in.

**Bridge to build:** "Home internet is too rigid for my life" → "home internet can be recharge-shaped" → "Wiom IS that."

**Must NOT do (L2):**
- Sound like "cheaper broadband" — they sense the trap and bounce
- Hide the recharge model — the recharge-shape IS the value prop for L2
- Add occasion examples before the structure lands — same sprint lesson
- Position as a downgrade ("if you can't afford broadband")
- Bury the granularity (1-day, 7-day, 30-day options are what unblocks L2 users)

---

### L3 — Workaround pain
"Mobile data for the house is painful." Convert mobile-data-pain users into home-internet consideration.

**Differentiator to land:** WHOLE-FAMILY-ON-ONE-RECHARGE. The math is the proof: 3–4 mobile recharges/week = 1 ghar recharge that serves everyone.

**Bridge to build (two steps):** Surface pain first → deliver ghar ka net as the relief that fits. "This pain is bearable, just life" → "this pain has a solution" → "the solution is ghar ka net."

**Must NOT do (L3):**
- Lead with category before pain — pain is the door for L3
- Shame current behaviour — mobile data has been their solution; respect before replacing
- Be aspirational — "imagine your family with home internet" is Bob's territory; keep it relief-based
- Compete with mobile providers — frame as "next level," not "alternative"
- Lead with price competition — about pain relief and the math, not "cheaper than mobile recharge"
- **CRITICAL L3 RULE:** "नेट" must be the destination noun. Never let "रिचार्ज" alone be the head noun. This rule applies IN-CONTEXT — a prior line, visual, or lockup that grounds the घर-का-नेट destination satisfies the rule. It is NOT applied per-line mechanically (a line can lead with recharge if a prior anchor has established the destination). Forbidden construction: "Wiom ka recharge karo" with no घर anchor anywhere in the creative.

---

### Cross-layer notes

**The universal load:** The word "recharge" arrives pre-loaded with the mobile-data schema (15+ years of mobile habits). Every creative across all three layers must attach "recharge" to घर का नेट — not leave it floating as a mobile-data-adjacent word.

**One job per creative (all layers):** When category and flexibility were both jammed in, neither landed. Land one thing cleanly, then the next in the next creative.

**Category-first, value-second (all layers):** When category lands, flexibility transmits. When category doesn't land, value cannot be acted on.

**Occasion examples (all layers):** Don't add use-case occasions (match, exam, coaching) before category is resolved. Audiences are still working out "what is this." Examples add cognitive load, not clarity. Exception: L2 occasion examples are permitted if and only if category anchors first.

## 9. Craft rules — from the May creative sprint (LOCKED)

These are rules, not preferences. Every brief obeys them. Source: Messaging System v1.3 §7.

| Rule | Evidence | What it means |
|------|----------|---------------|
| **Simple beats clever** | Static 01 (plain) = 60% Net id, 0% confusion. Static 02 (occasion examples) = 0% Net id, 21% confusion. | Remove occasion examples, comparisons, and multi-prop cramming until category is proved. |
| **One job per creative** | Multi-job creatives failed comprehension across the sprint. | Land category OR flexibility OR proof-of-commitment-shape — one per creative. |
| **Category-first, value-second** | When category landed (17/44), 10 also picked up flexibility. Reverse order failed. | Category must be established before the value prop is introduced. |
| **Zero confusion is the bar** | Static 01 + Static 03 both hit 0% confusion. | Not "under 20%" — zero. |
| **The benchmark is the winner** | Compare to best-in-class (Static 01), not to average. | If a new creative doesn't beat or match Static 01's comprehension, it hasn't earned deployment. |

## 10. JDRUDN / JZUR routing by constraint type

The correct anchor (JDRUDN vs JZUR) depends on the type of constraint the creative is addressing:

| Constraint type | Anchor to use | Rationale |
|----------------|---------------|-----------|
| **Cash constraint** (irregular income, salary delay, variable cashflow) | **JDRUDN** ("Jitne din recharge, utne din net") | Cash-constraint users need the RULE — they want to know they only pay when they have money. The operating logic is the relief. |
| **Need constraint** (short-duration need — exam, match, WFH days) | **JZUR** ("Jitni zarurat, utna recharge") | Need-constraint users need the BENEFIT — flexibility expressed as "exactly as much as you need." The value explanation is the relief. |

Do not swap these. A cash-constraint creative that uses JZUR (benefit) instead of JDRUDN (rule) undersells the operating logic that is the primary relief for that user.

All Wiom creatives follow a specific script-mixing rule. Evaluating a creative must account for this convention.

| Element | Script | Examples |

| **Forbidden** | Multi-word English phrases | "monthly bill", "family internet", "data finished" — not permitted |

## 12. AI-rendered vox-pop — credibility caveat

AI-generated vox-pop (e.g., Obvious AI renders) carries a credibility caveat that live-shot vox-pop does not:

- **Live vox-pop (VOX + LIV):** Credibility = realness of a real-public respondent. The "unscripted, candid" quality is what makes it work.
- **AI vox-pop (VOX + AI):** Credibility sits closer to peer/narrator character, NOT vox-pop-public credibility. If the AI face reads even slightly synthetic, it doesn't just score lower on credibility — it can contaminate the "real person" read the vox-pop format depends on.

When evaluating an AI vox-pop concept for G1/G2: assess it as a peer-character testimonial, not as a live-public vox-pop. The gate standards (ghar anchor, recharge-shape, no contamination) apply equally — but the credibility mechanism is different.

## 13. Verdict rules (PASS / ITERATE / KILL)

For the overall verdict, after both gates run:

- **PASS** — G1 passes and G2 passes. Concept moves to human approval → production-ready brief assembly.
- **ITERATE** — One or both gates fail on a structural-only issue that is fixable by a four-word add or single-line rewrite (e.g., missing ghar anchor at close, fixable; one kill-list word used as identity at close, fixable). Concept returns to the maker with a specific structured fix suggestion.
- **KILL** — One or both gates fail on a conceptual issue: the concept is **built on the wrong frame** (e.g., the entire creative is structured as a mobile-data top-up; the concept's premise is "Wiom is cheap WiFi"; the dangerous mobile-data-product misread is the most likely interpretation and a small edit cannot fix it). Concept is logged with reason, not iterated.

**Default bias is kill, not nurture.** Activation creative is cheap to produce; a marginal creative held alive "to see" accumulates contamination. A concept that does not clearly earn PASS or ITERATE is KILLed.

**Constraint-first reminder:** constraint-first story structure is the strongest current hypothesis but is **not a gate**. Do not fail a non-constraint-first creative on story structure alone.

**User-as-hero reminder:** user-as-hero is a strong Principle but is **not a Phase-1 gate**. Do not fail a creative on user-as-hero grounds in Phase 1. (Applied at the script-maker layer as an input constraint in Phase 2.)
